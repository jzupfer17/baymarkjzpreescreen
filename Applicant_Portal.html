Clinical Scenario

The patient is a 42 year old male with a long history of opioid use disorder, currently enrolled in a methadone maintenance program at 75 mg daily. He has a history of chronic back pain and recently underwent knee surgery, which has complicated his ability to work consistently. The patient also has untreated hypertension and type 2 diabetes, leading to occasional dizziness and fatigue. He reports ongoing anxiety and depressive symptoms, which have worsened since a recent divorce and the loss of custody of his youngest child. The patient struggles with unstable housing, frequently couch surfing with friends and relatives, and has limited social support. His substance use includes non prescribed benzos and some alcohol use, which he uses to cope with stress and chronic pain. He has had several prior hospitalizations for withdrawal and overdose, and he expresses a desire to reduce his methadone dose in the future but is currently ambivalent due to fear of pain and relapse.
